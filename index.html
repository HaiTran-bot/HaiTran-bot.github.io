About

I think in
clock cycles.

My name is Trần Tấn Hải and I'm a Computer Engineering student at Ho Chi Minh City University of Technology (HCMUT). I'm drawn to the part of computing most people never see — the logic gates, the register files, the waveforms on an oscilloscope.

My focus is digital design and computer architecture: implementing RISC-V CPUs from a blank Verilog file, understanding every hazard, every forwarding path, every memory access pattern. I don't use black boxes — I build them.

On the embedded side, I work close to the metal with STM32 microcontrollers — bare-metal C, peripheral drivers, RTOS scheduling — because a firmware engineer should understand the hardware they're programming, not just the HAL.
